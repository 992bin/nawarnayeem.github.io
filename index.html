About Me

I am a health economist and psychiatric epidemiology researcher. My work uses causal inference and machine learning to study suicidality, substance use, mental health disparities, and policy-relevant health outcomes.

I completed my PhD in Economics at Lehigh University and previously worked as a Postdoctoral Fellow at Saint Louis University. My research combines econometric and epidemiologic methods to generate evidence that improves public health decision-making.
