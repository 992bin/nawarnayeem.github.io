About

I am an applied economist and interdisciplinary health researcher with a PhD in Economics from Lehigh University. My work bridges health economics, psychiatric epidemiology, and machine learning to study suicidality, substance use, diagnosis, and disparities in mental health outcomes. I previously worked as a Postdoctoral Fellow at Saint Louis University and have taught health economics and microeconomics at the undergraduate and doctoral levels.

My academic mission is to develop rigorous, data-driven evidence that improves public health decision-making, strengthens clinical understanding, and informs equitable policy.
